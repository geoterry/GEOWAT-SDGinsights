Inequalities in water and sanitation access

When SDG monitoring is calling for methods and data sources for measuring inequalities in water, sanitation and hygiene (WASH) access, DHS modelled surfaces could provide a potential to reveal inequalities in services within countries. The inherent spatial nature of DHS modelled surfaces enable indicators to be disaggregated by specific geographic locations, such as subnational administrative areal units, urban and rural areas. By combining with supplementary geospatial data, indicators can be further disaggregated by other characteristics such as wealth.
